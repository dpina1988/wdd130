There are records of the practice of the Adonhiramite Rite in a previous period, between 1813 and 1815, in the first embryo of a Brazilian Masonic Power founded in Salvador, Bahia. In this sense, the Museum of the United Grand Lodge of England - UGLE, has in its collection an Adonhiramite ritual translated by Hipólito José da Costa, perhaps the most prominent Brazilian Freemason in Europe in the XIX century, which proves in a documental way the practice of the Adonhiramite Rite in Brazil even before we became an independent nation.

After the foundation of the Grand Orient of Brazil (GOB) on June 17th, 1822, the Adonhiramite Rite remains being practiced here in a traditional way and enjoys a period of ample growth, notably after the foundation of the Grande Oriente do Vale dos Beneditinos, or Grande Oriente do Passeio, whose Lodges would later be integrated to the Grand Orient of Brazil. Finally, in 2010 the Adonhiramite Rite made its way back to Europe, where it originated, and started being practiced again in Portugal.
