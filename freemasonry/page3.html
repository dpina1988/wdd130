What is Freemasonry? - Freemasonry is a philosophical, philanthropic, educational, and progressive institution.

Why is it Philosophical? -It is philosophical because its acts and ceremonies deal with the essence, properties, and effects of natural causes. It investigates the laws of nature and relates the first bases of morality and pure ethics.

Why is it Philanthropic? - It is philanthropic because it is not constituted to obtain personal profit for any class. On the contrary, its collections and resources are destined for the welfare of mankind, without distinction of nationality, sex, religion, or race. Furthermore, it seeks to achieve men's happiness through spiritual elevation and peace of conscience.

Why is it Progressive? - It is progressive because, starting from the principle of immortality and the belief in a regular and infinite creative principle, it does not cling to dogmas, prejudices, or superstitions. And it does not put any obstacle to the efforts of human beings in the search for truth, nor does it recognize any other limit to this search than that of reason based on science.

What are its principles? - The freedom of individuals and human groups, be they institutions, races, or nations; the equality of rights and obligations of beings and groups without distinguishing religion, race, or nationality; the brotherhood of all men, since we are all children of the same CREATOR and, therefore, human; and as a consequence, the brotherhood among all nations.

What is Freemasonry's motto? - Science - Justice - Work: Science, to enlighten the spirits and elevate them; Justice, to balance and enhance human relations; and work, through which men dignify themselves and become economically independent. In a word, Freemasonry works for the intellectual, moral, and social improvement of mankind.

What is its goal? - Its goal is the investigation of truth, the examination of morality, and the practice of virtues.

What does Freemasonry mean by morality? - Moral is, for Freemasonry, a science based on human understanding. The natural and universal law governs all rational and free beings. It is the scientific demonstration of conscience. And this fabulous science teaches us our duties and the reason for using our rights. By penetrating morality into the depths of our souls, we feel the triumph of truth and justice.

What does Freemasonry understand by virtue? - Freemasonry understands that virtue is the strength to do good in its broadest sense; it is the fulfillment of our duties to society and our family without personal interest. In summary: virtue does not recoil from sacrifice or even death when fulfilling the duty.

What does Freemasonry understand by duty? - Freemasonry understands by duty the respect and rights of individuals and society. But it is not enough just to respect property; we must also protect and serve our fellow men. Freemasonry summarizes the duty of man as follows: "Respect for God, love for your neighbor, and dedication to your family. Indeed, this is the greatest synthesis of universal brotherhood.

Is Freemasonry religious? - Yes, it is religious because it recognizes the existence of a single creative, regulating, absolute, supreme, and infinite principle, which is called the GREAT ARCHITECT OF THE UNIVERSE because it is a spiritualist entity in opposition to the predominance of materialism. These factors, which are essential and indispensable for a truly religious and logical interpretation of the UNIVERSE, form the basis and the great guidelines of all Masonic ideology and activity.

Is Freemasonry a religion? - No. Freemasonry is not a religion. It is a society that aims to unite men among themselves. Reciprocal union, in the broadest and highest sense of the term. And in its effort to unite men, it admits in its bosom people of all religious creeds without any distinction.

To be a Freemason, is it necessary to renounce the religion to which one belongs? - No, because Freemasonry shelters in its bosom men of any religion, as long as they believe in only one Creator, the GREAT ARCHITECT OF THE UNIVERSE, which is God. Generally, there is this belief among Catholics, but illustrious prelates have belonged to the Masonic Order; among others, Cura Hidalgo, Paladin of Mexican Freedom; Father Calvo, founder of Freemasonry in Central America; the Archbishop of Venezuela, Don Ramon Ignácio Mendez; Father Diogo Antonio Feijó; Canons Luiz Vieira, José da Silva de Oliveira Rolin, of the Inconfidência Mineira, Friar Miguelino, Friar Caneca, and many others.

What other distinguished men were Masons? - Philosophers like Voltaire, Goethe, and Lessing; Musicians like Beethoven, Haydn, and Mozart; Military men like Frederick the Great, Napoleon, and Garibaldi; Poets like Byron, Lamartine, and Hugo; Writers like Castellar, Mazzini, and Espling.

Were there illustrious Freemasons only in Europe? - No. They existed in America too. The liberators of America were all Masons. Washington in the United States; Miranda, the South American Freedom Father; San Martin and O'Higgins in Argentina; Bolivar in North South America; Marti in Cuba; Benito Juarez in Mexico and Emperor Dom Pedro I in Brazil.

What prominent names in Brazil were Masons? -D. Pedro I, José Bonifácio, Gonçalves Lêdo, Luis Alves de Lima e Silva (Duque de Caxias), Deodoro da Fonseca, Floriano Peixoto, Prudente de Morais, Campos Salles, Rodrigues Alves, Nilo Peçanha, Hermes da Fonseca, Wenceslau Braz, Washington Luiz, Rui Barbosa, and many others.

So Freemasonry is tolerant? - Freemasonry is eminently tolerant and demands the broadest tolerance from its members. It respects the. Political opinions and religious beliefs of all men, recognizing that all religions and political ideals are equally respectable and reject all pretensions of granting privileged status to any particular one.

What does Freemasonry fight? - Ignorance, superstition, fanaticism. Pride, intemperance, vice, discord, domination, and privilege.

Is Freemasonry a secret society? - No, for the simple reason that its existence is widely known. It has been granted legal personality by the authorities of several countries. Its purposes are widely spread in dictionaries, encyclopedias, history books, etc. The only secret that exists and is not known except by joining the institution is the means to recognize the Masons among themselves, in any part of the world, and the way to interpret their symbols and the teachings contained in them.

What are the indispensable individual conditions to be able to belong to Freemasonry? - To believe in the existence of a Creator principle; to be a free man with good morals; to be conscious of his duties to his country, his fellow men, and himself; to have a lawful and honorable profession or trade that allows him to provide for his personal needs and those of his family, and to support the works of the institution.

What is required of Freemasons? - In principle, everything that is required to join any other institution: respect for its statutes, regulations, and obedience to the resolutions of the majority, taken according to the principles that govern them; love for the Homeland; respect for the legally constituted governments; obedience to the laws of the country where you live, etc. And in particular: keeping the secrecy of the Masonic rituals; correct and dignified conduct inside and outside Masonry; dedicating part of your time to attend Masonic meetings; the practice of morality, equality, and human solidarity and justice in all its fullness. Moreover, it strictly forbids inside the institution political and religious discussions, because it prefers a broad base of understanding among men in order to avoid that they are divided by small issues of civil life.

What is a Masonic Temple? - It is a place where Masons gather periodically to practice the ritualistic ceremonies they are allowed in a fraternal and favorable environment to concentrate their attention and efforts to improve their character and their spiritual life, and to develop their sense of responsibility, making them calmly meditate on man's mission in life, constantly reminding them of the eternal values whose cultivation will enable them to get closer to the truth.

What do you get from being a Freemason? - The possibility of perfecting oneself, of learning, of disciplining oneself, of living with people who, by their words and deeds, can become examples and find fraternal affection wherever one is, inside or outside the country. Finally, the enormous satisfaction of having contributed, even in a small part, to the moral and great Work carried out by men. Freemasonry only considers progress possible on the basis of respect for personality, social justice, and the closest solidarity among men. It displays its motto, "Liberty, Equality, and Fraternity," with the abstention of political and religious flags. The Masonic secret, which in bad faith and slanderously has been used by its enemies to make it suspect among the candid or decadent spirits, is not a dogma but a procedure, a guarantee, a necessary and legitimate defense, but as has inevitably happened with every right and its correlative duty, the precept of Masonic reservations have already experienced its evolution through time and according to the countries. Freemasonry has no prejudice of powers and does not admit in its bosom people who do not have a minimum culture that allows them to practice their feelings and have a profession or income with which they can attend to the needs of their families, face the expenses of the society and help the needy.
